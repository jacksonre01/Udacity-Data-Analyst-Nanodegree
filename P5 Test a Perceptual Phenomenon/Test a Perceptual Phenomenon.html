(1) What is the independent variable? What is the dependent variable?

The independent variable is the word condition. The dependent variable is the time recorded response time to read through the word condition.

(2) What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task? What kind of statistical test do you expect to perform? Justify your choices.

Let congruent-condition equal the reading response time to the task in which the color word name and font color match
Let incongruent-condition equal the reading response time to the task in which the color word name and font color do not match

H0 - Null Hypothesis: ( congruent-condition = incongruent-condition ) The population mean reading response time for the congruent (congruent-condition) words is equal to the population mean reading response time for the incongruent (incongruent-condition) words.

HA - Alternative Hypothesis: ( congruent-condition incongruent-condition ) The population mean reading response time for the congruent (congruent-condition) words is significantly different from the population mean reading response time for the incongruent (incongruent-condition) words.

$$H_0: _{congruent-condition} = _{incongruent-condition}$$

$$H_A: _{congruent-condition} \neq _{incongruent-condition} $$

We have interval/continuous data, we are investigating the hypothesis of a difference in means, we have dependent samples, between two paired (matched) measures, and our data based on the charts below appears to be normally distrubuted therefore the appropriate test to use is the paired t-test. Additionally, z-tests are best used for larger samples where n>30. Our null hypothesis is that there is no difference between the mean response times for the congruent condition and the incongruent condition. # Perform the statistical test here
scipy.stats.ttest_rel(df['Congruent'], df['Incongruent'])
Out[84]:
Ttest_relResult(statistic=-8.020706944109957, pvalue=4.1030005857111781e-08)

Confidence level + alpha = 1. If we select alpha to equal .05, then we have 95% confidence level if our pvalue is less than alpha. If we allow alpha to be .01 then we have a 99% confidence level if our pvalue is less than alpha. Our t-statistic is -8.021 and our pvalue is extremely small (small pvalues are good) and less than .01 which indicates that the results did not occur by chance and that we have a better than 99% confidence level. Therefore we would reject our null hypothesis in favor of the alternate hypothesis that there is a statistically significant difference in the response rates.

Optional¶

The following Wiki article discusses some of the possible causes of this effect: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stroop_effect. Which in short are (1) related to the brain's processing speed, which may suggest that it reads words faster than it processes colors (2) that color recognition requires more attention (3) that color recognition is not an automatic response while the brain learns to automatically interpet the meanings of words.

In a related study, the effects of sleep deprvation were tested to see if they increased the response times associated with the Stroop Effect: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4418248/. It was found that the reaction times were significantly increased after 24hours of sleep deprivation when compared to the baseline.
